# Crystalward — Campaign Lore & Bosses

> **Canon story bible** · 5 levels × 10 phases · final phase = boss  
> **Tone:** *The Dark Crystal* × *Legend* — tactile dark fantasy, not random poetry  
> **Last updated:** 2026-07-17  

---

## 1. What is actually happening

### The Crystal of Truth

In the heart of **Thren** stands the **Castle of the Well**. Inside its chamber hangs the **Crystal of Truth** — a real physical object, not a metaphor. It regulates the flow of **essence**: the energy that keeps soil fertile, water clean, and living things from rotting into shadow-stuff.

When essence flows clean, day and night turn normally. Beasts sleep. Crops grow.

### The fracture

During the last **Great Conjunction** (three suns aligned over Thren), a seismic fault under the castle jarred the Crystal. A **hairline fracture** opened. Essence began to leak **wrong** — dark, sticky, half-alive. The locals call the corruption **umbrite**: essence that has gone stagnant and predatory.

Umbrite does three concrete things:

1. **Poisons guardians and beasts** — living things absorb it and become thralls (nightspawn).
2. **Animates dead wood, bone, and iron** — siege-things and walking husks.
3. **Weakens Lightwells** — smaller crystal-anchors the Wardens built so outer lands could still draw clean essence. If a Lightwell dies, that region loses its tether to the Castle Crystal and umbrite floods in permanently.

### The Lightwell

Each campaign map has a **Lightwell**: a tower-housing that holds a **shard-tether** (not the true Crystal, but a link to it). Nightspawn that reach it bite the tether. Too many bites and the region falls.

That is the game: protect the Lightwell through ten phases. Phase 10 is always Umbrael’s **champion** for that land.

### Lord Umbrael

**Umbrael** was once a high warden of the Castle Court — a real office, not a fairy-tale title. After the fracture he argued that the Crystal should be **dimmed on purpose**: less light, less leakage, a “managed dusk.” The Court refused. He broke with them, gathered umbrite-warped soldiers, and now marches to crack every Lightwell so the Castle Crystal is left alone, starving, easier to seize.

He is patient, armored in court silk gone black, and he does not joke about “night blooming.” He talks about **control**.

### Wardens

**Wardens** are the remaining oath-keepers: anyone who still binds essence into towers, garrisons, and weapons to hold the roads. No class system — the work is the oath.

## 2. Campaign structure

| | |
|---|---|
| **Levels** | **5** places (Vale → Crossing → Mire → March → Gate) |
| **Phases per level** | **10 sub-level maps** (playable stages — *not* combat waves) |
| **Waves** | Surges *inside* a map; few on early levels (~3–5), more later (~7–9) |
| **Boss stage** | Phase **10** of each level; its **final wave** is the boss alone |
| **Progression** | Linear: clear a map → unlock next map; clear phase 10 → next level |
| **Total maps** | 50 |

**Phase** = a distinct campaign map (e.g. L1–3 Mossbrook Bend).  
**Wave** = one enemy surge during that map.

## 3. The five lands and their bosses

Each boss is a **named being with a reason to exist** — corrupted local power or Umbrael’s appointed captain. Not random monsters.

---

### Chapter 1 — Homeland Vale  
**id:** `glade` · **look:** `homeland` · **lanes:** 1 · **boss:** `boss_harrow`

**Place:** Rolling meadows and light woodland on Thren’s eastern fringe — Gelfling-settled hills, pale paths, clean wells. This is where essence still mostly behaves.

**What the Soft Dark is doing here:** Scouting. Small thrall packs test the road. Umbrite is thin; it only clings to deep roots.

**Boss — Rootfather Harrow**  
An ancient **grove-warden spirit** bound into the great oak at the vale’s heart. For centuries it kept blight out of the soil. When umbrite first seeped through the fracture, Harrow tried to *drink* the poison to spare the meadows — and became a walking tree-colossus of blackened root and amber sap.

- **Moves:** Very slow. Enormous.  
- **Threat:** If it reaches the Lightwell it will *root into* the tether and choke it.  
- **Why phase 10:** Umbrael does not send an army first; he sends the land’s own guardian, already ruined, to prove the vale cannot protect itself.

---

### Chapter 2 — Twinveil Crossing  
**id:** `thorns` · **look:** `twinveil` · **lanes:** 2 (merge) · **boss:** `boss_fordwidow`

**Place:** Two tributaries of the Veil River meet at a stone ford. Mist rises from cold water. Trade and refugee paths join here before the inner woods.

**What is happening:** Umbrael needs the ford. Two columns of thralls force Wardens to split. Essence wells sit between the streams.

**Boss — The Ford-Widow**  
Once **Maera**, ford-keeper and bridge-priest, who rang the dusk bell so travelers crossed before dark. When umbrite fouled the rivers, she refused to abandon her post. The water took her body; what walks now is a tall, dripping figure of silt, reed-bone, and drowned bronze bells.

- **Moves:** Slow, relentless, along either approach until the merge.  
- **Threat:** High crystal damage — she knows how tethers work; she was trained to maintain them.  
- **Lore hook:** Her bells still toll when she takes a step. Wardens who grew up near the ford recognize the rhythm.

---

### Chapter 3 — Crosswind Mire  
**id:** `bog` · **look:** `mire` · **lanes:** cross / choke · **boss:** `boss_mirel`

**Place:** Peat marsh west of the rivers. Once a moss-temple district; now half-sunken. Dry ridges form the only safe roads. Night-blooming bog flowers are real plants that open for moths — and for thralls that use the scent as cover.

**What is happening:** Umbrael’s alchemists farm umbrite in the peat. Elites appear. The choke point is everything.

**Boss — Bog-Crown Mirel**  
**Mirel** was abbot of the moss-temple. He sealed umbrite into peat-cells “for study.” The cells failed. The peat rose around him into a **crown of living bog** — a mound-king that crawls on root-legs, trailing temple gold and drowned choir-masks.

- **Moves:** Slowest of the mid-game bosses; huge HP.  
- **Threat:** Siege damage to the Lightwell; shrugs ordinary slows somewhat (umbrite-sodden bulk).  
- **Why here:** He is not Umbrael’s general — he is Umbrael’s **mistake made flesh**, and Umbrael is happy to point him at the Wardens.

---

### Chapter 4 — Western March  
**id:** `march` · **look:** `march` · **lanes:** long dual · **boss:** `boss_vesk`

**Place:** Open dusk-heath and old military roads. Sparse cover. Two long approaches with little shared tower range. This is how armies reach the Castle.

**What is happening:** Full campaign logistics. Processions of ironclad and brutes. Essence is scarce. Lives are thin.

**Boss — Marshal Vesk**  
Umbrael’s **field marshal**, still mostly human under umbrite plate. He wears the black-and-rust armor of the old Castle Guard (his own former order). Vesk does not hate Wardens personally; he believes Umbrael’s “managed dusk” is the only way to stop the Crystal from bleeding the world dry.

- **Moves:** Slow armored advance; high HP; high crystal damage.  
- **Threat:** Will ignore distractions and walk the longest lane if it leads home.  
- **Lore hook:** May speak once when spawned: *“Stand aside. The Crystal is killing itself.”* He is wrong, but he believes it.

---

### Chapter 5 — Nightfall Gate  
**id:** `conjunction` · **look:** `gate` · **lanes:** 3 · **boss:** `boss_umbrael`

**Place:** Outer gate of the Castle of the Well. Three portal-roads. Violet stone. The true chamber’s light is visible as a glow beyond the walls. Conjunction approaches — suns align again; essence tides peak; umbrite surges with them.

**What is happening:** Final siege. Umbrael comes in person to break the last Lightwell and enter the Castle.

**Boss — Lord Umbrael**  
Not a metaphor. The traitor warden, swollen with decades of umbrite craft: taller than a garrison tower’s door, antler-crown of forged black crystal, court robes stiffened into armor. He is the strongest, slowest, most durable foe in the campaign.

- **Moves:** Deliberate. Three-path threat (spawns on a random portal).  
- **Threat:** Massive crystal damage — one leak is catastrophic.  
- **Win:** Umbrael falls; the Gate holds; the fracture does not heal forever, but the tether holds until the next age.  
- **Lose:** The Gate Lightwell dies; umbrite reaches the chamber; the Crystal’s song stops.

## 4. Sub-level (phase) structure (every level)

| Sub-levels | Content |
|---|---|
| 1–3 | Short maps; few waves; teach the land’s look and lanes |
| 4–6 | Longer; more waves; pressure rises |
| 7–9 | Hardest non-boss maps of the level |
| **10** | Boss stage — several prep waves, then **final wave = boss alone** |

Waves scale by **level number** first, then by how late you are in that level’s 10 maps.

---

## 5. Themes (concrete)

1. **Infrastructure of light** — Lightwells are technology/ritual hardware; losing them has geographic consequences.  
2. **Corruption of duty** — Harrow, Maera, Mirel, Vesk, Umbrael all *began* as caretakers.  
3. **Disagreement about salvation** — Umbrael’s plan is coherent and wrong; Vesk proves intelligent people can follow him.  
4. **Co-op as labor** — Two Wardens sharing one bank is how damaged infrastructure gets repaired under fire.
